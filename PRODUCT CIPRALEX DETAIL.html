CIPRALEX Cipralex is a brand name for a medication that contains aspirin as its active ingredient. Aspirin, also known as acetylsalicylic acid, is a widely used over-the-counter drug known for its analgesic, antipyretic, and anti-inflammatory properties. Below is a detailed overview of Dispirin: Composition Active Ingredient: Cipralex (acetylsalicylic acid) Form: Available as tablets, typically 300 mg or other doses depending on the formulation. Uses Dispirin is used to treat a variety of conditions, including: Pain Relief: Headaches, migraines Toothache Muscle and joint pain Menstrual cramps Fever Reduction: Used to lower body temperature in cases of fever. Anti-Inflammatory: Helps reduce swelling and inflammation in conditions like arthritis. Heart and Blood Vessel Protection: In low doses, aspirin is used to prevent blood clots, reducing the risk of heart attacks and strokes. Mechanism of Action Aspirin works by inhibiting the enzymes cyclooxygenase-1 (COX-1) and cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2). This reduces the production of prostaglandins and thromboxane, which are responsible for pain, inflammation, and blood clotting. Dosage and Administration For pain and fever: Typically 300–600 mg every 4–6 hours as needed. For cardiovascular protection: 75–100 mg daily, as prescribed by a doctor. Note: Always follow the recommended dose and consult a healthcare professional for proper guidance. Side Effects While generally safe when taken as directed, Dispirin can cause side effects, including: Stomach upset or irritation Heartburn Nausea and vomiting Increased risk of bleeding (due to blood-thinning effects) Allergic reactions (rash, difficulty breathing) Tinnitus (ringing in the ears) at high doses Contraindications Dispirin should not be used in the following cases: Allergy to Aspirin or NSAIDs Bleeding Disorders (e.g., hemophilia) Peptic Ulcer Disease Asthma (in sensitive individuals, it may trigger asthma attacks) Children or Teenagers with Viral Infections Associated with Reye's syndrome, a rare but serious condition. Precautions Avoid alcohol, as it may increase the risk of gastrointestinal bleeding. Use with caution in people with kidney or liver problems. Pregnant or breastfeeding women should consult a doctor before use. Storage Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Keep out of reach of children. Conclusion Dispirin is a versatile medication for pain relief, fever reduction, and heart health when used correctly. However, it is important to use it as directed and consult a healthcare provider for specific conditions or prolonged use.
