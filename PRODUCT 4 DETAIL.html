DECLORAN Decloran-D is a pharmaceutical formulation often used to relieve symptoms associated with allergies, cold, or sinus congestion. It is a combination medication containing two active ingredients, Desloratadine and Pseudoephedrine, which work together to address multiple symptoms. Composition: Desloratadine: A long-acting antihistamine that reduces the effects of natural histamine in the body. It helps relieve symptoms like sneezing, runny nose, itchy or watery eyes, and skin rashes. Pseudoephedrine: A decongestant that works by shrinking the blood vessels in the nasal passages, leading to reduced nasal congestion. Uses: Decloran-D is indicated for the relief of: Nasal congestion due to colds or allergies. Sneezing and runny nose. Sinus pressure or pain. Symptoms of hay fever (allergic rhinitis). Dosage: Typically taken once or twice daily, depending on the formulation and severity of symptoms. It should be taken with a full glass of water and can be taken with or without food. Do not crush or chew extended-release tablets. Note: The exact dosage and duration should be prescribed by a healthcare professional based on individual needs and age. Precautions: Allergies: Inform your doctor if you are allergic to desloratadine, pseudoephedrine, or any other similar medications. Medical Conditions: Consult your doctor if you have: Heart disease or high blood pressure. Glaucoma. Thyroid disorders. Kidney or liver issues. Diabetes. Pregnancy and Breastfeeding: Use during pregnancy or breastfeeding only if recommended by a doctor. Interactions: Avoid combining with monoamine oxidase inhibitors (MAOIs) or other decongestants. Side Effects: Common side effects may include: Dry mouth. Dizziness or drowsiness. Headache. Insomnia (due to pseudoephedrine). Rare but serious side effects: Increased heart rate. High blood pressure. Severe allergic reactions like swelling or difficulty breathing (seek immediate medical attention). Storage: Store at room temperature, away from direct sunlight and moisture. Keep out of reach of children. Important Notes: Avoid alcohol while taking Decloran-D, as it may enhance drowsiness. Do not exceed the recommended dosage. If symptoms persist for more than 7 days, consult your healthcare provider.
